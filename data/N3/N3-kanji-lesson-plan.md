>
> **Cross-reference note:** 3 kanji (的, 無, 公) are classified as N4 by Kanshudo/Nihongo-Pro but are not in our N5/N4 curriculum. They are included here to ensure complete coverage.

## Notes for Implementation

1. **Lesson 1 anchoring:** 忘/覚 (forget/remember) is an ideal opener — directly relevant to the act of studying Japanese, and creates an immediate emotional connection ("don't forget what you've learned!").

2. **Early-use vocabulary unlocks:** Lessons N3.46 (昨/次) and N3.48 (末) will finally unlock the kanji forms of きのう, つぎ, and 週まつ — words students have been writing in hiragana since N5.

3. **Thematic coherence priority:** Blocks 1–9 have strong thematic coherence. Blocks 10–15 are more loosely grouped — some kanji resist neat categorization. Individual lessons can be reshuffled within blocks if better themes emerge during content creation.

4. **N3.86 (3 kanji):** The final lesson has only 3 kanji. Options: (a) keep it light as a wind-down, (b) merge with N3.85 for a 7-kanji finale, or (c) redistribute one kanji from an earlier lesson.

5. **Grammar lessons:** N3 will need its own grammar lesson series (G24+). These should be planned separately after the kanji/vocabulary scope is finalized.

6. **Glossary:** A `glossary.N3.json` will need to be created with entries for all vocabulary using these 343 kanji, plus compound words that combine N3 kanji with previously taught N5/N4 kanji.
