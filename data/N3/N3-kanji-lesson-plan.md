# JLPT N3 Kanji Lesson Plan

> **Status:** Planning draft — frequency reorder applied
> **Total new kanji:** 348 (excluding 27 already taught in N5+N4)
> **Lessons:** 86 (80 × 4 kanji + 5 × 5 kanji + 1 × 3 kanji)
> **Source:** Composite of [JLPTsensei](https://jlptsensei.com/jlpt-n3-kanji-list/), [Nihongo-Career](https://nihongo-career.com/tips/2025/10/15/jlpt-n3-kanji-complete-list-and-learning-methods/), [JLPT Samurai](https://jlptsamurai.com/2025/03/08/jlpt-n3-kanji-list-380-essential-characters-master-the-key-kanji-for-n3-success/), [Kanshudo](https://www.kanshudo.com/collections/jlpt_kanji), [Nihongo-Pro](https://www.nihongo-pro.com/kanji-pal/list/jlpt)
>
> **Cross-reference note:** 3 kanji (的, 無, 公) are classified as N4 by Kanshudo/Nihongo-Pro but are not in our N5/N4 curriculum. They are included here to ensure complete coverage.
>
> **Reorder rationale:** N3.1–N3.13 are locked (already scoped/built). N3.14–N3.26 front-load 13 high-frequency lessons that were originally scattered across N3.41–N3.86 — each scored 5–7 on a compound frequency analysis (理由, 自由, 良い, 最近, 助ける, 準備, 実は, 置く, 頼む, 優しい, 笑う, 他の, 目的, 気付く). N3.27–N3.86 contain the remaining 60 lessons in their original relative order, preserving thematic flow while accepting the later placement.

## Notes for Implementation

1. **Locked zone (N3.1–N3.13):** These lessons are already scoped/built and must not be reordered. They front-load the highest-frequency vocabulary unlocks — words students have been writing in hiragana since N5 (友達, 昨日, 次, 一緒, 週末) plus game-essential kanji (命, 戦, 守, 殺).

2. **Frequency front-load (N3.14–N3.26):** 13 lessons pulled forward from N3.41–N3.86 based on compound frequency analysis. Each lesson scored 5–7 on a scale measuring high-utility word unlocks. The biggest wins: 良い (N3.15, was 86), 最近/最も/最初 (N3.16, was 63), 助ける/準備/完成 (N3.17, was 75), 理由/自由 (N3.14, was 85).

3. **Remaining order preserved (N3.27–N3.86):** The 60 lessons not pulled forward retain their original relative order. This preserves thematic flow (emotions cluster, physical actions cluster, communication cluster, etc.) while accepting the later placement. The highest-frequency remaining lesson is 断/決/定/予 (Decisions & Plans) at N3.29 — only pushed back 13 slots from its original N3.16.

4. **Managed kana gaps:** Some compounds have kanji split across distant lessons due to the reorder:
   - 突然 (突 N3.18, 然 N3.84) — write とつぜん until N3.84
   - 絶対 (絶 N3.15, 対 N3.57) — write ぜったい until N3.57
   - 原因 (因 N3.19, 原 N3.84) — write げんいん/原いん until N3.84
   All managed via the standard partial-kanji writing system.

5. **Compound integrity preserved:** All intra-lesson compound fixes from the regrouping pass are intact: 警察(N3.55), 番号(N3.58), 当然(N3.84), 性格(N3.60), 関係(N3.74), 処置(N3.20).

6. **Grammar lesson compatibility:** Grammar lessons G29–G46 unlock after specific N3.X numbers. The reorder changes which kanji are taught at each unlock point, but grammar lessons don't depend on specific kanji being available — they teach grammatical concepts. No grammar lesson is broken by this reorder. Note: ぜんぜん (G36 adverbs of degree, unlocks after N3.26) remains in kana since 然 arrives at N3.84.

7. **Early-use vocabulary unlocks:** N3.3 (昨/次) unlocks きのう → 昨日 and つぎ → 次. N3.5 (末) unlocks 週まつ → 週末. N3.4 (緒) unlocks いっしょ → 一緒.

8. **Pronoun upgrade:** N3.6 (彼/君/誰) gives students 彼 and 彼女 plus 誰 — high-frequency pronouns previously stuck in hiragana.

9. **5-kanji lesson positions:** N3.6, N3.56, N3.57, N3.60, N3.84. The 3-kanji lesson is N3.73 (経/組/給).

10. **Grammar lessons:** N3 will need its own grammar lesson series (G24+). These should be planned separately after the kanji/vocabulary scope is finalized.

11. **Glossary:** A `glossary.N3.json` is being built incrementally as lessons are scoped. Entries for all vocabulary using these 348 kanji, plus compound words that combine N3 kanji with previously taught N5/N4 kanji.
