# 台風の思い出
## Memories of the Typhoon

---

### Story Text (Japanese)

朝、りきぞは台風が来ると知った。「台風が来たら、何をしなければいけないんだ」とりきぞは思った。すぐにやまかわに知らせるために電話をした。「台風のことを知って いるか」とりきぞが言った。「知って いるよ。計画を考えれば、だいじょうぶだよ」とやまかわが言った。二人は台風のための計画を考えながら、りきぞの家に集まった。

二人は台所に行った。台所で食べ物を集めたり、時計を見ながら台風が来る時間を計ったりした。「今まで以上に風が強くなるよ」とやまかわが言った。「台所以外の物も集めなければいけないね。去年以下にならないと思うよ」とりきぞが言った。やまかわは特に集中しながら、色々な物を集めていった。二人は台風が来るよりも前に、たくさんの物を集めることができた。

台風が終わったら、二人は外に出た。近所の人も集まってきた。「台風の時、だいじょうぶだったか」とやまかわがりきぞに聞いた。「うん、計画を考えたから、だいじょうぶだった」とりきぞが答えた。やまかわはりきぞに色々な質問をした。りきぞは答えながら、この台風のことがとても特別だと思った。

その夜、りきぞは今日のことをゆっくり思い出した。台風のこと、二人で考えた計画のこと、近所の人が集まったこと。「この思い出は、とても大切だ」とりきぞは思った。時計を見たら、もう夜になって いた。「つぎに台風が来たら、もっとよく計画を考えられるようにしよう」とりきぞは思った。りきぞの心の中に、台風の思い出が大きくなってきた。

**おわり**

---

### English Translation

**Memories of the Typhoon**

One morning, Rikizo learned that a typhoon was coming. "If a typhoon comes, what do I have to do!" he thought. He immediately called Yamakawa in order to let him know. "Do you know about the typhoon?" said Rikizo. "I know. If we think of a plan, we'll be fine," said Yamakawa. The two of them gathered at Rikizo's house while thinking of a plan for the typhoon.

The two went to the kitchen. In the kitchen, they gathered food and measured the time until the typhoon would arrive while watching the clock. "The winds are going to get even stronger than before," said Yamakawa. "We have to gather things from outside the kitchen too. I don't think it will be weaker than last year," said Rikizo. Yamakawa was especially concentrated as he systematically gathered all sorts of things. Before the typhoon arrived, the two were able to gather a great many things.

When the typhoon was over, the two of them went outside. The neighbours also gathered together. "Were you okay when the typhoon hit?" asked Yamakawa. "Yeah, we had a plan, so it was fine," Rikizo answered. Yamakawa asked Rikizo all sorts of questions. While answering, Rikizo felt that this typhoon experience was something very special.

That night, Rikizo slowly recalled the events of the day. The typhoon, the plan the two of them had worked out together, the neighbours who had gathered. "This memory is very precious," Rikizo thought. When he looked at the clock, it had already become night. "Next time a typhoon comes, I'll make sure I'm able to plan even better," he thought. Inside Rikizo's heart, the memory of the typhoon had grown bigger and bigger.

**The End**
