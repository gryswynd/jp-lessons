# 旅先の写真

---

りきぞは一人で旅行に行くまえに、家でカメラを見ました。自分の目で世界を見るために、この旅は一人だけで行きたかったです。けんはやさしい友だちです。旅行のまえに、けんにいいプレゼントをもらいました。けんは「山の有名な旅館はいい所ですよ。」と言いました。去年の旅行より、この旅のほうが大切でした。カメラは手首にありました。「行きましょう。」と、家を出ました。

電車に乗って、山のほうに行きました。電車の中から、白いゆきの山がよく見えました。ゆきの山は、世界の中心のように見えました。本で見た山より、ここの山のほうが大きかったです。りきぞは手首のカメラで、外の山を写しました。川の真ん中も白くて、きれいでした。「自分の目で見るほうがいいですね。」と、小さく言いました。

旅館に着いた時、外はとても寒かったです。ゆきが多くて、山も川も真っ白でした。有名な旅館はとても古くて、大きかったです。旅館の人はやさしくて、りきぞはお茶をもらいました。「外は寒いですから、お茶をどうぞ。」と、その人は言いました。「ありがとうございます。」と言いました。旅館の人は、家族みたいにやさしかったです。旅館の中で、お茶を飲みながら、外の白い山を見ました。

つぎの朝、りきぞは旅館を出て、山の道を歩きました。朝の山はとても明るかったです。山の道を歩きながら、写真をとったり、川を見たりしました。川に着くまえに、古い大きい木も写しました。川の真ん中がきれいだったので、カメラで写真をとりました。カメラだけで、たくさんとりました。「川の中心は、きれいですね。」と、小さく言いました。

道を歩いていた時、旅人に会いました。その旅人は世界中を旅した人で、とても頭がいい人でした。旅人の顔はやさしくて、頭の中にはたくさんの旅先がありました。その人は有名人でした。先生のように、やさしく話しました。「旅先では色々な物が学べますよ。」と、その人は言いました。りきぞは「自分の目で世界を見たかったです。」と、言いました。旅人は「自分の目で見るほうが、本より大切ですよ。」と、また言いました。

晩ご飯を食べてから、旅人はりきぞに旅先でとった写真を見せてくれました。その写真は、旅人が世界中で写した写真でした。りきぞは写真を見ながら、旅人の旅を聞きました。写真の旅人の顔色はよくて、首も体も元気でした。「旅の中心は心ですよ。写真より、心のほうが大切ですよ。」と、旅人が言いました。りきぞは旅人に、川の真ん中の写真をあげました。旅人はりきぞに、いい物をたくさんくれました。

旅行の後で、りきぞは家に帰ってから、写真を見ました。たくさん歩きましたけど、心はうれしかったです。写真の中の自分の顔を見ました。前の自分の顔より、この旅の顔のほうが、いい顔でした。けんに会って、旅の物と写真を見せました。「いい旅でしたね。つぎの旅は、二人で行きましょう。」と、けんは言いました。写真より、旅で学んだ心のほうが、大切でした。

---

### English Translation

**Photos at the Destination**

Before going on a solo trip, Rikizo carefully looked over his camera at home. In order to see the world with his own eyes, he wanted to make this trip alone. Ken is a kind friend. Before the trip, Rikizo received a nice present from Ken. Ken had said, "The famous mountain inn is a really wonderful place." Compared to last year's trip, this trip felt more important. The camera was on his wrist. "Let's go," he said, and left the house.

He boarded the train and headed for the mountains. From inside the train, he could clearly see the white snowy mountains. The snowy peaks looked like the center of the world. The mountains here were bigger than the ones he had seen in books. With the camera on his wrist, Rikizo captured the mountains outside. He took many shots. The middle of the river was also white and very beautiful. "Seeing things with your own eyes really is better," Rikizo said softly.

When he arrived at the inn, it was very cold outside. There was lots of snow, and the mountain and river were pure white. The famous mountain inn was very old and very large. The innkeeper was kind, and Rikizo received a cup of tea. "It's cold outside, so please have some tea," they said. "Thank you," he said. The innkeeper was as kind as anyone in the world. Inside the inn, sipping tea, he watched the white mountains outside.

The next morning, Rikizo left the inn and walked along the mountain road. The morning mountain was very bright. As he walked, he took photos and looked at the river. Before reaching the river, he photographed an old, large tree on the mountainside. Because the middle of the river was so beautiful, he took its picture with his camera. With only his camera, he took many photos. "The center of the river really is lovely," he said softly.

While walking on the road, he met a traveler. That traveler was someone who had journeyed across the world and was very wise. The traveler's face was gentle, and his eyes were kind. Inside his head were many travel destinations. He turned out to be a famous person — someone the whole world recognized. He spoke gently, like a teacher. "At travel destinations, you can learn many things," he said. Rikizo replied, "I wanted to see the world with my own eyes." The traveler said, "Seeing it with your own eyes is more important than any book."

After dinner, the traveler showed Rikizo photos he had taken at his destinations. They were photos he had captured all across the world. While looking at them, Rikizo listened to the traveler's stories. The traveler's complexion in the photos was good, and his neck and body looked full of life. "The center of a journey is the heart. More than photos, the heart matters most," the traveler said. Rikizo gave him a photo of the middle of the river. From the traveler, Rikizo received many good things in return.

After the trip, once Rikizo had returned home, he looked at his photos many times. He had walked a lot, but his heart was happy. He looked at his own face in the photos. More than his face from before, his face on this trip looked better. He met Ken and showed him the things and photos from the trip. "What a wonderful trip," Ken said. "Next time, let's go together — the two of us," Ken added. More than the photos, the heart he learned about during the trip was deeply important.

**The End**
