# 森の食堂
## The Forest Dining Hall

---

### Story Text (Japanese)

ある朝、やまかわはりきぞに「都市の近くの森の中に、古い食堂があるんだ。習字を習うことができるよ」と言った。りきぞはそんな食堂に行ったことがなかったので、とても行きたいと思った。二人は用事を終わらせてから、森へむかった。

森の食堂に着いたら、堂々とした大きい所が目に入ってきた。「この食堂は何時代から開いているんだろう」とりきぞは思った。食堂の先生は引き出しから紙を引き出して、りきぞに習字を見せてくれた。「ここでは何時代も前から、この習字の学習が大切にされてきたんだよ。世代から世代へとね」と先生が言った。りきぞは先生に習字の書き方を教えられ、書くことを習わされた。

食事をしながら習字を書いたり、先生の書き方を見習ったりした。「学習のためには、毎日少しずつ習うことが大切だ」と先生が言った。食事が終わってから、りきぞとやまかわは食堂の近くで開かれた朝市へ行った。朝市で食べ物や用事のための物を買ったり、森林の中を歩いたりした。「こんなに楽しい朝市は、今まで知らなかったんだ」とりきぞは思った。

夕方になってきたら、二人は帰ることにした。「また来たいと思う。習字も朝市も、ほんとうに楽しかったね」とりきぞが言った。「この森の食堂は、これからもあってほしいと思うよ」とやまかわが言った。りきぞは今日の学習がとても大切だったと思った。つぎに来る時は、もっと上手に習字が書けるようにしようと思った。

**おわり**

---

### English Translation

**The Forest Dining Hall**

One morning, Yamakawa told Rikizo, "There's an old dining hall deep in the forest near the city. You can learn calligraphy there." Since Rikizo had never been to a dining hall like that before, he thought he'd really like to go. After finishing their errands, the two of them headed toward the forest.

When they arrived at the forest dining hall, a grand and magnificent place came into view. "From what era has this dining hall been open, I wonder," Rikizo thought. The dining hall's teacher pulled paper out from a drawer and showed Rikizo the calligraphy. "Here, the study of calligraphy has been treasured since many eras ago — passed down from generation to generation," said the teacher. Rikizo was taught the way to write calligraphy by the teacher, and was made to practice writing.

They ate while writing calligraphy and followed the teacher's example. "For the sake of studying, it is important to learn a little each day," said the teacher. After the meal was finished, Rikizo and Yamakawa went to the morning market that was held near the dining hall. At the morning market, they bought food and things for their errands, and also walked through the forest. "This is the first time I've ever come to such an enjoyable morning market," thought Rikizo.

As the evening came, the two decided to head home. "I want to come again, I think. Both the calligraphy and the morning market were really fun," said Rikizo. "I think I want this forest dining hall to be here forever," said Yamakawa. Rikizo thought that today's studying had been very important to him. Next time he came, he thought he would aim to write calligraphy even more skillfully.

**The End**
