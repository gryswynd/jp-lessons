# 夕方の村
## Evening in the Village

---

### Story Text (Japanese)

りきぞは七夕を心待ちにして いた。山村へ行くために、「校門の前で待とうよ。校門が待ち合わせの所だ」とりきぞがやまかわに言った。「分かった。早く来れば、いっしょに林の中を歩けるよ」とやまかわが言った。りきぞは七夕のことを何度も考えながら、校門の前に着いた。待合の所には、もう村人たちもいた。

やまかわが来たら、二人は正門を出て山村へ進んだ。林の中の道を歩いて いったら、池の近くに花があった。「この山林はとてもきれいだな」とやまかわが言った。「村人たちがあつまっているよ。夕方になったら、七夕が始まるんだ」とりきぞが言った。山村に着いたら、村人たちがいっしょにあつまって いた。今度はいっしょに来ることができてよかったと、りきぞはおもった。

夕方になると、七夕が始まった。二人は夜空を見ながら、歌の声を聞いて いた。「一度来たら、また来たいよ」とやまかわが言った。「そうだよ。毎年来るようにしなければいけないね」とりきぞが言った。りきぞの心の中に、楽しい気持ちが大きくなってきた。村の方が山の中よりも好きになってきた。

夕飯の時間になった。村人の料理はりきぞの口に合って、とてもおいしかった。「毎年来たいな」とやまかわが言った。「今度また来るようにしよう」とりきぞが言った。夕飯を食べてから、二人はいっしょに帰った。りきぞは何度でもこの村に来たいとおもった。

**おわり**

---

### English Translation

**Evening in the Village**

Rikizo had been looking forward to the Tanabata festival. In order to go to the mountain village, "Let's wait in front of the school gate. The school gate is our meeting spot," said Rikizo to Yamakawa. "I understand. If you come early, we can walk together through the grove," said Yamakawa. Rikizo arrived at the school gate while thinking about Tanabata many times. At the meeting spot, the villagers were already there too.

When Yamakawa arrived, the two of them left through the main gate and proceeded toward the mountain village. As they walked along the path through the grove, there were flowers near the pond. "This mountain forest is very beautiful," said Yamakawa. "The villagers are gathering. When it becomes evening, the Tanabata will begin," said Rikizo. When they arrived at the mountain village, the villagers had gathered together. Rikizo felt it was good to have been able to come together this time.

When evening arrived, the Tanabata festival began. The two of them were listening to the voices singing while looking at the night sky. "Once you come here, you'll want to come back," said Yamakawa. "That's right. We have to make sure to come every year," said Rikizo. A joyful feeling grew inside Rikizo's heart. He came to like the village side more than deep in the mountains.

It became time for dinner. The villagers' cooking suited Rikizo's taste and was very delicious. "I want to come every year," said Yamakawa. "Let's make sure to come again next time," said Rikizo. After eating dinner, the two of them went home together. Rikizo felt that he wanted to come to this village as many times as possible.

**The End**

### Grammar Points Used

| Pattern | Example from story | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 心待ちにする (anticipation) | 七夕を心待ちにして いた | was looking forward to the Tanabata festival |
| ために (in order to) | 山村へ行くために | in order to go to the mountain village |
| ～ば conditional | 早く来れば、いっしょに林の中を歩けるよ | if you come early, we can walk together in the grove |
| ～ながら (while doing) | 七夕のことを何度も考えながら | while thinking about Tanabata many times |
| ～たら conditional | やまかわが来たら、二人は正門を出た | when Yamakawa arrived, the two left |
| ～と natural-result conditional | 夕方になると、七夕が始まった | when evening came, the Tanabata began (automatic sequence) |
| ～ていく (continuing onward) | 歩いていったら | as they walked along the path (directional te-form + tara) |
| ～てくる (gradual change) | 楽しい気持ちが大きくなってきた | a joyful feeling grew (came to grow) |
| ～になってくる (come to become ~) | 村の方が好きになってきた | gradually came to like the village more |
| なければいけない (must) | 毎年来るようにしなければいけないね | we have to make sure to come every year |

| より (comparison) | 村の方が山の中よりも好き | liked the village side more than the mountains |
| のです / んだ (explanatory) | 七夕が始まるんだ | the Tanabata festival is going to start (explanatory) |
